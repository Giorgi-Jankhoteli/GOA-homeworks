Mercedes-Benz CLS

The Mercedes-Benz CLS
(initially called the CLS-Class) is a series of executive cars produced by Mercedes-Benz from October 2004 to August 2023.
The original model was a four-door sedan based on the Mercedes E-Class platform, marketed as a four door coupé. An estate (shooting brake) model was later added to the model range with the second generation CLS.All models are available as a high performance AMG variant, although it wasn't until the second generation CLS that 4MATIC all-wheel drive was offered.
The CLS range is positioned between the E-Class and the S-Class within the Mercedes model range, and models tend to be less practical than the E-Class it is based on. It primarily competes with other fastback sedans like the BMW 8 Series Gran Coupe, Porsche Panamera and Audi A7. Production of the CLS ended on 31 August 2023 to prepare for the launch of the new Mercedes-Benz E-Class (W214).

First generation (C219; 2004)

The CLS, marketed by Mercedes as a four-door coupé, was designed by Michael Fink in 2001. Known as the C219 in Europe and W219 in the US, it shares its platform with the W211 E-Class, including engines and a wheelbase of 2,854 mm (112 in). IVM Automotive developed the production version from the Vision CLS concept, which debuted at the 2004 New York International Auto Show.

Second generation (C218; 2010)

The C218 CLS, the second generation sold from 2011 to 2018, includes a 4-door coupé (C218/W218) and a 5-door estate (X218, marketed as 'Shooting Brake'). Its design drew inspiration from the Mercedes F800 concept and features elements from the SLS AMG. Unlike its predecessor, the C218 offers all-wheel drive, especially on CLS 63 AMG models. A facelift in 2014 introduced design updates, engine enhancements, and the Mercedes 9G-Tronic automatic transmission.

Third generation (C257; 2018)

The C257 CLS is the third generation, available as a four-door coupé with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive (4MATIC). Although a Shooting Brake version was in development, it was discontinued due to declining demand. Engine options include a 2.9-liter straight-six turbocharged diesel and a 3.0-liter straight-six turbocharged petrol, with the latter powering the CLS 53 AMG, which features a mild hybrid system and a Panamericana grille reminiscent of the 300 SL. Unlike its predecessors, the C257 is a five-seater.
